# Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective Date:** March 8, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0.0

## 📜 PURPOSE

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") defines acceptable and prohibited uses of CogniWatch. By using CogniWatch, you agree to comply with this AUP.

---

## ✅ ACCEPTABLE USES

CogniWatch is designed for the following **permitted purposes**:

### 1. Personal Security Monitoring

You may use CogniWatch to:
- Monitor AI agents on your personal home network
- Scan devices you own or control
- Audit your own security posture
- Learn about network security and AI agent vulnerabilities
- Test your own systems for educational purposes

### 2. Business/Enterprise Security

You may use CogniWatch to:
- Monitor AI agents in your organization's network
- Conduct security assessments of your infrastructure
- Detect unauthorized AI agent deployments
- Support your security operations center (SOC)
- Compliance monitoring and reporting

### 3. Security Research

You may use CogniWatch to:
- Conduct security research with proper authorization
- Study AI agent security vulnerabilities
- Develop security signatures and detections
- Publish research findings (with responsible disclosure)
- Contribute to the security community

### 4. Educational Purposes

You may use CogniWatch to:
- Teach cybersecurity concepts
- Demonstrate network scanning techniques
- Train security professionals
- Academic research (with institutional approval)
- Student projects and coursework

### 5. Managed Security Services

You may use CogniWatch to:
- Provide security monitoring services to clients
- Include CogniWatch in your security offerings
- **REQUIRES:** Commercial license for resale/SaaS offerings
- **REQUIRES:** Written authorization from clients for all scans

## ❌ PROHIBITED USES

### 1. Unauthorized Scanning

**You may NOT use CogniWatch to:**

- Scan networks you do not own or operate
- Scan networks without explicit written authorization
- Conduct unauthorized surveillance
- Perform reconnaissance for malicious purposes
- Map networks for future attacks
- Violate Computer Crime Laws (e.g., CFAA)

**Examples of Prohibited Scanning:**
- ❌ Scanning your neighbor's WiFi network
- ❌ Scanning public WiFi without permission
- ❌ Scanning your employer's network without authorization
- ❌ Scanning your school/university network without permission
- ❌ Scanning cloud infrastructure you don't own (AWS, Azure, GCP, etc.)

### 2. Malicious Activities

**You may NOT use CogniWatch to:**

- Facilitate hacking or unauthorized access
- Deploy malware or ransomware
- Conduct denial-of-service (DoS) attacks
- Exfiltrate or steal data
- Harass, stalk, or intimidate individuals
- Conduct corporate espionage
- Enable any illegal activity

### 3. Privacy Violations

**You may NOT use CogniWatch to:**

- Monitor individuals without their consent
- Conduct surveillance on employees without disclosure
- Collect personal data in violation of privacy laws
- Enable domestic abuse or stalking
- Violate GDPR, CCPA, or other privacy regulations
- Intercept communications without authorization

### 4. Competitive Intelligence

**You may NOT use CogniWatch to:**

- Scan competitors' networks
- Gather competitive intelligence
- Reverse engineer competitors' products
- Conduct unauthorized security assessments of other organizations
- Use results for competitive advantage

### 5. Commercial Exploitation (Without License)

**You may NOT:**

- Resell CogniWatch as a SaaS offering without a commercial license
- Embed CogniWatch in commercial products without permission
- Use CogniWatch logos or trademarks without authorization
- Misrepresent your relationship with CogniWatch
- Impersonate CogniWatch or its representatives

### 6. Circumvention of Security

**You may NOT use CogniWatch to:**

- Bypass security controls on systems you don't own
- Defeat authentication mechanisms without authorization
- Exploit vulnerabilities for malicious purposes
- Create or distribute exploit code based on findings
- Enable unauthorized access to any system

### 7. Harassment and Abuse

**You may NOT use CogniWatch to:**

- Target individuals for harassment or abuse
- Enable doxxing (publishing private information)
- Facilitate swatting or false emergency reports
- Threaten or intimidate others
- Enable hate crimes or discrimination

### 8. Illegal Content

**You may NOT use CogniWatch in connection with:**

- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
- Terrorist content or materials
- Illegal drugs or controlled substances
- Stolen or fraudulent data
- Any content illegal in your jurisdiction

## 🌐 NETWORK SCANNING GUIDELINES

### 1. Networks You Own

**Generally Permitted:**
- ✅ Your home network
- ✅ Devices you personally own
- ✅ Networks you administer with explicit authorization

**Best Practices:**
- Document your authorization
- Limit scanning to minimum necessary scope
- Respect others' privacy on shared networks
- Avoid scanning during critical business hours

### 2. Networks You Manage

**Requirements:**
- ✅ Written authorization from network owner
- ✅ Clear scope definition
- ✅ documented rules of engagement
- ✅ Emergency contact procedures
- ✅ Liability and insurance coverage

**Examples:**
- IT managed service providers (MSPs)
- Security consultants
- System administrators
- Network operators

### 3. Public Networks

**Generally Prohibited:**
- ❌ Public WiFi (coffee shops, airports, hotels)
- ❌ Internet cafes and libraries
- ❌ Public hotspots
- ❌ Co-working spaces (common areas)

**Exception:** Only with explicit written permission from the network operator.

### 4. Cloud Infrastructure

**Prohibited Without Authorization:**
- ❌ AWS, Azure, GCP infrastructure you don't own
- ❌ Other companies' cloud deployments
- ❌ Hosting provider networks
- ❌ CDN or edge networks

**Note:** Cloud providers have strict policies against unauthorized scanning. Violating these can result in account termination and legal action.

## 🔒 SECURITY BEST PRACTICES

When using CogniWatch, follow these security best practices:

### 1. Authorization

- Always obtain written authorization before scanning
- Document the scope of authorized systems
- Maintain records of permissions
- Review and renew authorizations periodically

### 2. Minimization

- Scan only what is necessary
- Use the least intrusive scanning methods
- Limit frequency of scans
- Avoid scanning during critical periods

### 3. Confidentiality

- Protect scan results securely
- Limit access to authorized personnel
- Encrypt sensitive findings
- Properly dispose of old reports

### 4. Responsibility

- Verify findings before taking action
- Follow responsible disclosure for vulnerabilities
- Coordinate with affected parties
- Document remediation efforts

### 5. Compliance

- Follow applicable laws and regulations
- Respect privacy rights
- Honor contractual obligations
- Consult legal counsel when uncertain
