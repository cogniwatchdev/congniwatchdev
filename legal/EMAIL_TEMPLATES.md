## 1. SECURITY VULNERABILITY REPORT

**When:** Someone reports a security vulnerability via email or GitHub Security Advisories  
**Priority:** HIGH (respond within 24 hours)  
**To:** security@cogniwatch.dev

---

**TEMPLATE:**

```
Subject: Re: Security Vulnerability Report - [Brief Description]

Dear [Reporter Name],

Thank you for responsibly disclosing this security concern to CogniWatch. We take 
security seriously and appreciate your effort to protect our community.

INITIAL ASSESSMENT:
───────────────────
Vulnerability Type: [Type]
Severity: [Critical/High/Medium/Low]
Affected Versions: [Versions]
CVE Assignment: [Pending/Assigned - CVE-YYYY-XXXXX]

NEXT STEPS:
───────────
1. Our team is investigating this report (timeline: 3-5 business days)
2. We will provide a status update by [DATE - 7 days from now]
3. If confirmed, we will develop and test a patch
4. Coordinated disclosure after fix is available

WHAT WE NEED FROM YOU:
──────────────────────
[ ] Can you provide additional reproduction steps?
[ ] Are you available for follow-up questions during remediation?
[ ] Do you wish to be credited in the security advisory (yes/no)?
[ ] Preferred credit name: [If yes]

SAFE HARBOR:
────────────
As outlined in our Security Policy, we provide safe harbor for good-faith 
security research. You will not face legal action for responsible disclosure.

TIMELINE:
─────────
• Initial Response: Within 48 hours ✅ (this email)
• Status Update: Within 7 days
• Remediation: 30-90 days (depending on severity)
• Public Advisory: After patch is available

We will be in touch soon with our findings. In the meantime, please do not 
disclose this vulnerability publicly until we have had time to remediate.

Thank you again for helping keep CogniWatch secure.

Best regards,
CogniWatch Security Team
security@cogniwatch.dev
https://cogniwatch.dev/legal/security-policy

---
CogniWatch — Security Monitoring for Self-Hosted AI Agents
🔒 Report security issues: security@cogniwatch.dev
```

## 5. TRADEMARK / BRAND CONCERN

**When:** Someone objects to our use of their logo/trademark, or asks about using ours  
**Priority:** HIGH (respond within 24-48 hours)  
**To:** legal@cogniwatch.dev

---

**TEMPLATE (THEY OBJECT TO OUR USE):**

```
Subject: Re: Trademark Concern - [Trademark Name]

Dear [Contact Name],

Thank you for reaching out regarding the use of [Trademark Name] in CogniWatch. 
We take intellectual property rights seriously.

OUR USE:
────────
CogniWatch is a security scanning tool that detects AI agent frameworks on 
local networks. We display framework names and logos to accurately identify 
what we detect — this is nominative fair use, standard in the security industry.

Examples of similar tools: Shodan, Nessus, Qualys, Nmap

YOUR CONCERN:
─────────────
We understand your concern about [specific issue raised]. We want to assure you:
• We do not claim endorsement or partnership
• We use logos as-is, without modification
• We clearly identify ourselves as a third-party tool
• We link to your official documentation (driving traffic to you)

RESOLUTION OPTIONS:
───────────────────
1. Keep current use with trademark disclaimer (our preference)
2. Use text-only identification (no logo)
3. Remove reference entirely (last resort)

TRADEMARK DISCLAIMER:
─────────────────────
We're happy to add a disclaimer such as:
"[Trademark] is a trademark of [Company]. Used for identification purposes only 
under nominative fair use. CogniWatch is not affiliated with or endorsed by [Company]."

OUR GOAL:
─────────
We respect your brand and want to find a solution that works for everyone. Our 
intent is to accurately identify detected frameworks, not to mislead users.

Can we discuss this further? We're open to your suggestions for appropriate 
attribution that addresses your concerns while allowing us to function as a 
detection tool.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Legal Counsel, CogniWatch
legal@cogniwatch.dev
```

## 📊 EMAIL MANAGEMENT BEST PRACTICES

### Response SLAs

| Email Type | Target Response | Max Response |
|------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Security vulnerability | 24 hours | 48 hours |
| Legal / C&D | 24 hours | 48 hours |
| Press inquiry | 24 hours | 48 hours |
| Support question | 24 hours | 72 hours |
| Commercial licensing | 2-3 days | 5 days |
| Partnership | 3-5 days | 7 days |
| Feature request | 3-5 days | 10 days |
| Bug report | 24 hours | 48 hours |

### Labeling System

Use email labels/tags for tracking:
- `security` — Escalate to security team
- `legal` — Requires legal review
- `press` — Communications team
- `support` — General support queue
- `sales` — Commercial inquiries
- `bug` — Engineering review needed
- `feature` — Product team review

### Escalation Paths

- **Security:** security@cogniwatch.dev → Security team Slack channel
- **Legal:** legal@cogniwatch.dev → Attorney (if serious)
- **Press:** press@cogniwatch.dev → Founder/CEO for interviews
- **Commercial:** sales@cogniwatch.dev → Business development

### Tools to Use

- **Email Templates:** Save in Gmail/Outlook templates or use helpdesk software
- **Ticketing:** Consider GitHub Issues, Zendesk, or Freshdesk for tracking
- **Auto-responder:** Set up for all contact addresses
- **Shared Inbox:** Use support@, legal@, security@ as shared mailboxes
