Privacy Policy

Effective: March 8, 2026 • Version 1.0.0

🔒 YOUR PRIVACY

Your privacy is critically important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how CogniWatch ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your information when you use our software.

1. DATA COLLECTION

1.1 Information We Collect

CogniWatch is designed to run locally on your infrastructure. We minimize data collection:

Information We DO NOT Collect:

  • Personal information (name, email, address)
  • Payment information
  • Network scan results or findings
  • AI agent telemetry data
  • System configurations or credentials

Information We MAY Collect (if you choose to share):

  • Usage Analytics: Anonymous usage statistics (if enabled via opt-in)
  • Error Reports: Crash reports and error logs (if enabled)
  • Support Communications: Communications when you contact us for support

2. HOW WE USE INFORMATION

If you opt-in to analytics or error reporting, we use this information to:

  • Improve software functionality and performance
  • Identify and fix bugs
  • Understand feature usage patterns
  • Enhance security and prevent abuse
  • Provide customer support

3. DATA STORAGE AND SECURITY

3.1 Local Storage

All scan results, agent discoveries, and configuration data are stored locally on your infrastructure in:

  • SQLite databases
  • Local configuration files
  • Memory (temporary runtime data)

3.2 No Cloud Storage

CogniWatch does not upload your scan results, network data, or agent information to cloud servers. All data remains under your control.

3.3 Security Measures

We implement reasonable security measures to protect the software, including:

  • Input validation and sanitization
  • Protection against injection attacks
  • Rate limiting on API endpoints
  • Secure session management
  • Regular security updates

4. DATA SHARING

4.1 Third-Party Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your data to third parties.

4.2 Service Providers

We may use third-party services for analytics or error reporting (only if you opt-in). These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data.

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law or to protect our rights, comply with legal processes, or ensure the safety of users.

5. COOKIES AND TRACKING

5.1 Essential Cookies

The dashboard may use essential cookies for session management and security purposes.

5.2 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies are used only if you explicitly opt-in. You can disable them at any time.

6. DATA RETENTION

6.1 Local Data

You control retention of local data (scan results, logs, configs). Configure retention settings in the dashboard.

6.2 Analytics Data

If you opt-in to analytics, anonymized usage data may be retained indefinitely for product improvement.

7. YOUR RIGHTS

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights including:

  • Access to your personal data
  • Correction of inaccurate data
  • Deletion of your data
  • Restriction of processing
  • Data portability
  • Object to processing

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@cogniwatch.dev.

8. CHILDREN'S PRIVACY

CogniWatch is not intended for users under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

9. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

As CogniWatch runs locally, data typically does not leave your infrastructure. If you opt-in to cloud services (analytics, support), data may be transferred to jurisdictions with different data protection laws.

10. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify users of material changes via email (if provided) or through prominent notices in the software.

11. GDPR COMPLIANCE

For EU users, CogniWatch is designed to comply with GDPR requirements:

  • Lawful Basis: Legitimate interests (security monitoring) and consent (analytics)
  • Data Minimization: We collect only what's necessary
  • Purpose Limitation: Data is used only for specified purposes
  • Storage Limitation: Data is retained only as long as needed
  • Integrity and Confidentiality: Appropriate security measures are implemented

12. CCPA COMPLIANCE

For California residents, CogniWatch complies with CCPA requirements:

  • Right to Know: You can request information about data collection
  • Right to Delete: You can request deletion of personal data
  • Right to Opt-Out: You can opt-out of data sales (we don't sell data)
  • Non-Discrimination: All rights are exercised without discrimination
