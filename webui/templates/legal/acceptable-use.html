Acceptable Use Policy

Effective: March 8, 2026 • Version 1.0.0

✅ ACCEPTABLE USE GUIDELINES

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") outlines permitted and prohibited uses of CogniWatch software. By using CogniWatch, you agree to comply with these guidelines.

1. PERMITTED USES

1.1 Authorized Network Scanning

You may use CogniWatch to:

  • Scan networks, systems, and services you own or operate
  • Scan networks with explicit written authorization from the owner
  • Detect AI agents and frameworks within your infrastructure
  • Monitor security posture of systems under your control
  • Identify vulnerabilities in your own networks
  • Conduct security assessments for clients (with proper authorization)

1.2 Internal Business Use

You may deploy CogniWatch within your organization for:

  • IT security monitoring
  • Compliance auditing
  • Asset discovery and inventory
  • AI governance and oversight
  • Security research and development

1.3 Research & Development

You may use CogniWatch for:

  • Security research in controlled lab environments
  • Testing your own systems and applications
  • Educational purposes and training
  • Contributing improvements to the CogniWatch project

2. PROHIBITED USES

⛔ STRICTLY PROHIBITED

The following uses of CogniWatch are strictly forbidden and may result in termination of your license and potential legal action.

2.1 Unauthorized Access

You may NOT use CogniWatch to:

  • Scan networks, systems, or services you do not own without explicit authorization
  • Access systems or data without proper credentials or permissions
  • Circumvent security controls or authentication mechanisms
  • Conduct unauthorized penetration testing
  • Perform reconnaissance for malicious purposes

2.2 Malicious Activities

You may NOT use CogniWatch to:

  • Harvest credentials, personal information, or sensitive data
  • Conduct surveillance or espionage
  • Enable harassment, stalking, or doxxing
  • Support criminal activities of any kind
  • Facilitate unauthorized data exfiltration
  • Launch attacks against systems (DoS, DDoS, etc.)

2.3 Commercial Exploitation

You may NOT use CogniWatch to:

  • Offer CogniWatch as a managed service without a commercial license
  • Resell or sublicense CogniWatch to third parties
  • Embed CogniWatch in commercial products without written permission
  • Use CogniWatch for competitive intelligence gathering

2.4 Compliance Violations

You may NOT use CogniWatch in ways that violate:

  • Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  • Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
  • Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)
  • Any applicable local, state, national, or international laws

3. RESPONSIBLE USE REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Authorization Documentation

When scanning third-party systems, you must:

  • Obtain written authorization before scanning
  • Maintain records of authorization
  • Limit scans to authorized scope and timeframe
  • Report findings only to authorized parties

3.2 Data Protection

You must:

  • Protect any data collected during scans
  • Encrypt sensitive scan results
  • Limit access to scan results to authorized personnel
  • Delete data when no longer needed
  • Comply with applicable data protection laws

3.3 Ethical Conduct

You should:

  • Act in good faith and with integrity
  • Respect privacy and confidentiality
  • Disclose vulnerabilities responsibly
  • Minimize impact on scanned systems
  • Report security issues to appropriate parties

4. ENFORCEMENT

4.1 Monitoring Compliance

We may monitor use of CogniWatch to ensure compliance with this AUP. This may include:

  • Reviewing usage patterns
  • Analyzing telemetry data (if enabled)
  • Investigating reports of misuse
  • Cooperating with legal authorities when required

4.2 Violations

Violations of this AUP may result in:

  • Written warning and opportunity to cure
  • Immediate termination of license
  • Denial of access to future updates
  • Legal action for damages
  • Reporting to law enforcement (for illegal activities)

4.3 Appeal Process

If you believe your license was terminated in error, you may appeal by contacting legal@cogniwatch.dev with:

  • Explanation of the situation
  • Evidence supporting your position
  • Corrective actions taken (if applicable)

5. REPORTING VIOLATIONS

If you become aware of violations of this AUP, please report them to:

  • Email: abuse@cogniwatch.dev
  • Anonymous Reports: Available upon request

All reports will be investigated confidentially and promptly.

6. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

We may update this Acceptable Use Policy at any time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of CogniWatch after changes constitutes acceptance of the new terms.

7. RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER AGREEMENTS

This AUP is incorporated into and subject to the CogniWatch Terms of Service and other applicable agreements. In case of conflict, the more restrictive provision applies.
